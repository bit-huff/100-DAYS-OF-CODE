Semantic Tags

What are Semantic tags?

Semantic tags add meaning to your HTML. They tell both the browser and developer what kind of content is being presented.

Example:

  • header: Contains introductory content
  • nav: Holds navigation links
  • main: Signifies the main content area of webpage
  • article: Encapsulates a self-contained composition
  • figure/ficaption: Used for embedding images, diagrams, or charts along with caption
  • section: Represents a thematic grouping of a webpage content
  • aside: Contains content aside from the content it is placed in
  • footer: Holds footer information

Why use Semantic tags?

They enhance SEO, improve accessibility and make your code easier to read and maintain.
